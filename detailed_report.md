
## 2006 FIFA World Cup Summary

**Champion:** Italy
**Runner-Up:** France

### Summary:
The 2006 FIFA World Cup, hosted by Germany, saw Italy emerge victorious after a penalty shootout against France in the final. The tournament was marked by high-profile controversies, including Zinedine Zidane's headbutt and subsequent red card in the final. Miroslav Klose of Germany won the Golden Boot with 5 goals. The World Cup was well-organized and attended, with an average attendance of over 52,000 per match. It was viewed as a success for Germany, enhancing the nation's image and fostering a sense of national pride.
